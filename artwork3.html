The Kiss

The Kiss is a mixed-media installation that depicts two large 3D printed hands holding digital screens which display faces with eyes closed held up to each other simulating a kiss. This work dissects the fostering of human relationships and connection through digital outlets and how, in this digital age, technology acts as an extension of oneself. Though an intimate scene is being depicted, it’s artificially constructed, which touches upon the fact that people can make connections through technology, even with this element of physical barrier and simulation. Thus showing how human connection is being shaped in a digital era.
